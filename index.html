01 — ABOUT

Engineering the Future
of Embodied AI

I'm a Robot Software Engineering Lead with a deep-rooted passion for advanced manipulation, adaptive control, and physical hardware. With a B.Sc. in Physics from DDUC, Delhi University and an M.Sc. in Informatics from IIC, Delhi University, paired with over five years of hands-on experience in the robotics industry — from high-throughput warehouse automation at Addverb to cutting-edge consumer robotics at Clutterbot — I have developed a sharp intuition for the friction between software intent and hardware reality.

My career is defined by technical evolution. I have driven the transition from classical motion planning architectures (PID, MoveIt) to data-driven autonomy (Reinforcement Learning, Vision-Language-Action models). I don't just train models or write simulation scripts; I build the teams and the robust C++/ROS2 infrastructures required to deploy these models reliably onto physical actuators and sensors in unstructured environments. I'm proficient in C++, deeply experienced with ROS/ROS2, Linux, and communication protocols like RS485, CAN, EtherCAT, and Modbus.

I thrive on solving "unsolved" problems at the cutting edge, turning complex theoretical AI into tangible, household-ready robotic actions.

04 — INTERESTS

Philosophy & Curiosity

The Hardware-Software Interface

Endlessly fascinated by low-level embedded systems and the physical realities of robotics. A beautiful algorithm is useless if the actuator cannot handle the torque. I spend a significant amount of time studying custom PCB designs, sensor fusion integrations, and the mechanical limits of modern servo motors.

Mentorship & Engineering Culture

I believe the best engineering environments are built on psychological safety and continuous learning. Since 2017, I have served as a mentor and resource person for university robotics clubs (including my alma mater, DDUC), conducting workshops and guiding students from foundational concepts to complex ROS deployments.

Pushing the Boundaries of Teleoperation

Beyond autonomous models, I am actively exploring VR-based teleoperation and high-fidelity force-feedback systems. Bridging the gap between human intuition and robotic execution is a critical stepping stone toward gathering the high-quality data necessary to train the next generation of general-purpose robots.
